The Real Good

Oh, the real good is the kindness That turns the neighbor's night to day, And the real warmth is the blindness That wipes the neighbor's faults away. For the good is always better When it's fashioned, like the Master's plan, To warm another's weary shelter, And not just to feed one man.

The true light is the reflection Of the mercy that we give, For in the depths of our affection We glimpse the light by which we live. And the faith that breathes forgiveness, Like the incense from a holy pyre, Will show the world the path to heaven Is the path of sweet desire.

So let us give the real good In the only way we can, For the treasure of a brotherhood Is the measure of a man.
